**🔴 Short Thesis (3 Pillars):**

- **Unprecedented Denosumab Biosimilar Onslaught:** The FDA has approved **nine pairs of biosimilars** referencing Prolia and Xgeva. Six have already launched commercially, with more expected throughout 2026. This represents the most concentrated biosimilar assault on any single originator product in FDA history. Hikma launched Enoby and Xtrenbo in January 2026; Teva's Ponlimsi approved March 30, 2026.

- **Revenue Erosion Accruing Across Portfolio:** Prolia/Xgeva biosimilars, Enbrel decline, and Otezla Medicare price-setting pressure create simultaneous headwinds across Amgen's three largest legacy products. Analysts at Guggenheim describe Amgen as "a company in transition" — but the pace of legacy erosion is accelerating faster than pipeline replacement.

- **Pipeline Attrition Undermining Replacement Thesis:** Rocatinlimab (OX40) and bemarituzumab (FGFR2b) have experienced pipeline setbacks that reduce confidence in Amgen's ability to replace denosumab revenue within the critical 2-3 year window. Repatha, Tezspire, and Imdelltra are growing but face their own competitive pressures.

**🔴 Short Squeeze / Upside Risks:**
